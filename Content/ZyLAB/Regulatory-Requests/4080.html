Interviews

After a request for information or a raid, a company often takes actions to catch up with the authorities.

These actions consist of two options:
1. Interviews with potential stakeholders.
2. An internal investigation in which data is collected and analyzed.

Important questions that need to be answered include, for example:

- Who were involved?

- What do they know

- Do they have relevant electronic data or communication in their possession?

- How was it handled?

- Did (middle) management know about it?

- Have there been any warning signs? What has been done with that?

- Was there guidance from management?

- Etc.

Who are involved? What happened? When did it happen? Why did it happen? Where did it happen?
Who were involved from the management and the sales? What happened? When did the violation start? Why did the violation happen? Where did the violation happen?
Who of the management received early warning signals? Has there been guidance during the misconducts from the management? How long did the violations happen? Why did certain prices increase? Who was where?
Who else knew about the competition violations? How did the violations happen? At what moment did the management find out about the misconducts? Information about prices charged to particular customers?  
Who suffered from the violations? Did the management take immediate actions to prevent further violations? Did the violation stop at all?  
Who are the competitors? Was anything done to cover up the misconducts?  
Which companies supplied which amounts to (key) customers? Explanation of concerning contacts with competitors, evolution of prices and relevant turnovers?
