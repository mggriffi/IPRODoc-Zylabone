Common Entity Type Entity Sub type Description
PTM Named Entity Extractor
Street and Number ADDRESS1 Corresponds to the first part of a postal address. At least two pieces of the following address information must be adjacent to be extracted as ADDRESS1: Building name, House number, Street name, Apartment number, PO Box and number, Neighborhood. The format is typical of the country (or countries) where the language is spoken
Postal code and City ADDRESS2 Corresponds to the second part of a postal address. At least two pieces of the following address information must be adjacent to be extracted as ADDRESS2: Postal code, City, State/province/department. Country names are always extracted as COUNTRY, even in an address context.
Continent CONTINENT The name of any continent.
Country COUNTRY The name, or the abbreviated name of a country.
Money CURRENCY A quantity of world currency, or a range of amounts of currency.
Date DATE A date is minimally composed of a number and month.
Day DAY A day of the week, including abbreviations.
Facility FACILITY A man-made structure.
FACILITY@AIRPORT The name of a primarily man-made or man-maintained structure whose primary use is as an air transportation terminal.
FACILITY@BUILDGROUNDS The name of an architectural or civil engineering structure, or an outdoor space that is mainly man-made or man-maintained. There is no distinction with respect to their function, they could be civil or military facilities, they could be used for work or entertainment, or they could be monuments.
FACILITY@PATH The name of a primarily man-made or man-maintained structure that allows fluids, energy, persons, animals, or vehicles to pass from one location to another.
FACILITY@PLANT The name of a facility composed of one or more buildings used for industrial purposes.
FACILITY@SUBAREA The name of a portion of a facility, typically an architectural one, that is able to contain people, animals, or objects.
Geographic Area GEO_AREA A geographical area larger than a city and consisting of a significant land mass, such as a group of countries.
GEO_AREA@DOMESTIC The name of a location that does not cross national boundaries.
GEO_AREA@INTL The name of a location that crosses national boundaries.
GEO_FEATURE A geographical location that does not constitute a political entity and is not a CONTINENT, GEO_AREA, COUNTRY, REGION, or LOCALITY.
GEO_FEATURE@CELESTIAL The name of an astronomical location that is outside of the boundaries of the Earth
GEO_FEATURE@BOUNDARY The name of a location such as a border.
GEO_FEATURE@LAND The name of a location that is a geologically or ecosystemically designed, nonartificial location.
GEO_FEATURE@WATER The name of a location that is a body of water.
Geographic Coordinates GEOCOORD Geographic coordinates, of various formats.
Holidays HOLIDAY Holidays when banks and businesses are closed (bank holidays) in countries where that language is spoken. Spelled out dates that are also names of holidays are extracted as HOLIDAY (like &quot;Fourth of July&quot;) whereas numeric dates that coincide with a holiday are extracted as DATE (like,&quot;4th of July&quot;).
Language LANGUAGE The name of a language.
Locality LOCALITY Name of a city.
Measure MEASURE A measure expression, or a range expression denoting measure.
Month MONTH The name of a month of the year, including abbreviations.
NIN NIN@CA_SIN A Canadian Social Identification number.
NIN@CH_AVS A Swiss national identification number: Assicurazione Vecchiaia e Superstiti.
NIN@FR_INSEE A French national identification number: Institut National de la Statistique et des Études Économiques.
NIN@IT_CF An Italian national identification number: Codice Fiscale.
NIN@KR_RRN A Korean resident registration number (??????).
NIN@NL_BSN A Dutch national identification number: Burgerservicenummer.
NIN@US_SSN National identifier used in the USA : Social Security Number. Any extracted NIN/US_SSN entities can be parsed and standardized using the Data Cleanse transform of Data Services by mapping them to one of the SSN input fields.
Organization ORGANIZATION@COMMERCIAL The name of commercial organizations, such as major companies or corporations. Any extracted ORGANIZATION/COMMERCIAL entities can be parsed and standardized using the Data Quality Data Cleanse transform by mapping them to one of the FIRM input fields.
ORGANIZATION@EDUCATIONAL The name of an institution focused primarily on education.
ORGANIZATION@ENTERTAINMENT The name of any organization, whether nonprofit or for profit, whose main interest is in the production of performing arts material or events: dance, music, opera, theatre, magic, spoken word, circus arts, and musical theatre.
ORGANIZATION@GOVERNMENT The name of an organization related to government, politics, or the state.
ORGANIZATION@MEDIA The name of any organization, whether nonprofit or for profit, at any level, whose main interest is in the production of publishing and/or radio-, TV-, cable-, satellite-, or web-broadcasting material.
ORGANIZATION@MEDICALSCIENCE The name of an organization focused on medical care or research.
ORGANIZATION@RELIGIOUS The name of an organization focused on religion.
ORGANIZATION@SPORTS The name of an organization focused on sports.
ORGANIZATION@OTHER The name of any organization that does not fit into a more specific subtype, including groupings of geopolitical entities that can function as political entities.
People PEOPLE A name referring to an identifiable group of people based on nationality. In certain languages, ethnicity, region, or religion are also recognized.
Percent PERCENT A percent expression, or a range expression denoting percentage.
Person PERSON An individual specified by name. A variety of forms are identified. Given and family names that occur by themselves are extracted as PERSON as long as they are not ambiguous with common names, with the exception of famous cases, such as Bush.
Phone PHONE A telephone number based on the patterns used in the USA and internationally.
Product PRODUCT A product name, optionally preceded by a company name.
Geographic Area REGION@MAJOR The major administrative division of a country, such as a state of the US, including abbreviations, a province or territory of Canada, the regions of England, and so on.This list also includes names of geo-political entities for which conventional labels do not apply, such as disputed territories or territories that have not been internationally recognized.
REGION@MINOR The name of a county, prefecture, district, or analogous geographical division or governmental unit.
Twitter ID SOCIAL_MEDIA@ID_TWITTER A Twitter handle or ID starting with &quot;@&quot;.
Twitter Topic SOCIAL_MEDIA@TOPIC_TWITTER A Twitter topic name: a character sequence starting with &quot;#&quot;.
Ticker TICKER A company stock ticker symbol used on the stock exchange. If TICKER entities are found adjacent to index or market names, these are included in the span.
Time TIME A clock time or a time expression.
TIME_PERIOD A measure of time duration or an expression denoting a range of measures of time.
Title TITLE The name of a position in government, business, or some other organization.
Email URI@EMAIL An e-mail address, including Lotus Notes addresses.
IP URI@IP An Internet Protocol address.
URL URI@URL A uniform resource locator, or web address.
Vehicle VEHICLE@AIR An air vehicle, such as an airplane or helicopter.
VEHICLE@LAND A land vehicle, including the color, year, model and make of the vehicle.
VEHICLE@WATER A water vehicle.
VEHICLE@LICENSE An alphanumeric sequence that conforms to the US and Canadian license plate formats, when preceded by a state abbreviation.
VEHICLE@VIN A Vehicle Identification Number (VIN), which always consists of 17 characters. Each position in the VIN has a particular meaning, designating among other things county code, manufacturer code, equipment code, serial number, and so on.
Weapon WEAPON@BIOLOGICAL The name of some bacteria, virus, fungus, natural toxin, or disease that has been officially identified as used to harm humans, plants (crops), and animals, or as a potential biological threat. This also extracts entities that describe the means for the dispersal of any of these weapons.
WEAPON@CHEMICAL The name of a chemical substance that has been officially identified as used to harm humans, plants (crops), and animals, or as a potential chemical threat. This also extracts entities that describe the means of the dispersal of any of these weapons.
WEAPON@EXPLODING The name of a substance or weapon that is designed or used to explode for the purpose of causing damage.
WEAPON@NUCLEAR The name of a weapon that has been officially identified as used to harm humans, plants (crops), and animals through the dispersal of radiological or nuclear energies, or has been identified as a potential nuclear threat.
WEAPON@PROJECTILE The name of a weapon that is designed or used to be projected at great speed for the purpose of causing damage.
WEAPON@SHOOTING The name of a weapon that is designed or used to send projectile objects at great speed for the purpose of causing damage.
Year YEAR A year identifier.

Strong negative emoticon StrongNegativeEmoticon Extracts emoticons conveying strong negative sentiment, e.g. :-(((, :((, :'-(
Profanity Ambiguous Ambiguous_profanity Extracts words and phrases that are pejorative only in certain contexts
Profanity Unambiguous Unambiguous_profanity Extracts words and phrases that are always pejorative, regardless of the context
